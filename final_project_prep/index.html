Dwayne The Rock Johnson

From Wrestling to Hollywood

Rise to Glory

Dwayne Douglas Johnson was born on May 2, 1972, into a family of professional wrestlers — his father Rocky Johnson and grandfather Peter Maivia were both legends in the ring. But his rise wasn’t easy. After facing challenges in school and early failures in football, Johnson turned to wrestling in 1996. Under the name Rocky Maivia, he was initially rejected by the crowd. But with time, he evolved into The Rock, a bold, charismatic superstar with unmatched mic skills. By the early 2000s, The Rock had become one of WWE’s most iconic and beloved figures, winning multiple championships and captivating millions worldwide. His larger-than-life personality and unmatched work ethic laid the foundation for his future success beyond the ring.
