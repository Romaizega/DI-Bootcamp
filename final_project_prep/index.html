Dwayne The Rock Johnson

From Wrestling to Hollywood

Rise to Glory

Dwayne Douglas Johnson was born on May 2, 1972, into a family of professional wrestlers — his father Rocky Johnson and grandfather Peter Maivia were both legends in the ring. But his rise wasn’t easy. After facing challenges in school and early failures in football, Johnson turned to wrestling in 1996. Under the name Rocky Maivia, he was initially rejected by the crowd. But with time, he evolved into The Rock, a bold, charismatic superstar with unmatched mic skills. By the early 2000s, The Rock had become one of WWE’s most iconic and beloved figures, winning multiple championships and captivating millions worldwide. His larger-than-life personality and unmatched work ethic laid the foundation for his future success beyond the ring.

On the Big Screen

"I didn't want to be a movie star — I wanted to be a great actor who just happened to do action films." — Dwayne Johnson

The Mummy Returns (2001)

Dwayne Johnson made his film debut as The Scorpion King in this action-packed sequel. His role, though short, introduced him to movie audiences worldwide. The character's popularity led to a solo spin-off.

IMDb: 6.4/10

The Scorpion King (2002)

In his first leading role, Dwayne Johnson stars as Mathayus, a fearsome warrior destined to become the legendary Scorpion King. Set in ancient times, the film follows his quest for revenge against a ruthless ruler, blending action and mythological fantasy.

IMDb: 5.5/10

The Rundown (2003)

In The Rundown, Dwayne Johnson plays Beck, a retrieval expert sent to the Amazon jungle to bring back a mobster’s son. The film mixes high-octane action with sharp humor, showcasing Johnson’s range as both a tough guy and a charismatic comedic lead.

IMDb: 6.6/10

Walking Tall (2004)

In Walking Tall, Dwayne Johnson stars as Chris Vaughn, a former soldier who returns to his corrupt hometown and takes a stand against crime and injustice. With a 2x4 in hand, he becomes the town's sheriff and restores order by force and willpower.

IMDb: 6.6/10

Doom (2005)

In Doom, Dwayne Johnson plays Sarge, the leader of a marine unit sent to a research facility on Mars to investigate a distress call. The movie is a dark, violent sci-fi horror adaptation of the iconic video game and features an intense first-person shooter sequence.

IMDb: 5.2/10

Gridiron Gang (2006)

In Gridiron Gang, Dwayne Johnson takes on the role of Sean Porter, a probation officer at a juvenile detention center who forms a football team to teach discipline, teamwork, and purpose to young inmates. Based on a true story, this emotional sports drama highlights Johnson’s dramatic range and inspiring screen presence.

IMDb: 6.9/10

Trophies & Triumphs

8× WWE Champion — One of the most iconic titleholders in WWE history, The Rock’s reigns defined an era of charisma, power, and unmatched mic skills.

Royal Rumble Winner (2000) — The Rock outlasted 29 other superstars to claim victory in the Royal Rumble match, earning a title shot at WrestleMania

10× Slammy Award Winner — Recognized for his charisma, promos, and in-ring excellence, The Rock won numerous Slammy Awards including Best Catchphrase and Match of the Year.

Hollywood Walk of Fame (2017) — Honored with a star for his outstanding contributions to film and entertainment, The Rock cemented his legacy in Hollywood.

Forbes Highest-Paid Actor (2019 & 2020) — With massive box office hits and brand deals, The Rock topped Forbes’ list of highest-paid actors two years in a row, earning global recognition as a business powerhouse.
